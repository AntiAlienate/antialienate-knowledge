# Tsimhoni v Eibschitz-Tsimhoni (Oakland County, Michigan, 2015) / Eibschitz-Tsimhoni v Tsimhoni (Mich. Ct. App., Docket Nos. 329406 (2016) and 336868 (2018))

**Court (trial):** Oakland County Circuit Court, Family Division (Michigan)
**Trial Judge:** Hon. Lisa O. Gorcyca (assigned to the matter for approximately five years)
**Date of the landmark hearing:** 24 June 2015 (children placed in Children's Village/"Mandy's Place" detention); release ordered approximately 9 July 2015
**Appellate Court:** Michigan Court of Appeals (unpublished opinions 2016 and 2018)
**Judicial-conduct case:** *In re Hon. Lisa O. Gorcyca*, Michigan Judicial Tenure Commission Formal Complaint No. 98; Michigan Supreme Court order on discipline, 2017
**Reported at:** [Justia – 2018 Mich. Ct. App. opinion (Docket 336868)](https://law.justia.com/cases/michigan/court-of-appeals-unpublished/2018/336868.html) | [Justia – 2016 Mich. Ct. App. opinion (Docket 329406)](https://law.justia.com/cases/michigan/court-of-appeals-unpublished/2016/329406.html) | [Courthouse News – release order](https://www.courthousenews.com/judge-releases-kids-she-jailed-in-divorce-case/) | [Detroit News – JTC misconduct finding](https://www.detroitnews.com/story/news/local/oakland-county/2016/07/01/judge-lisa-gorcyca-guilty-hearing/86609062/) | [Innocence Project – children released from juvenile detention](https://innocenceproject.org/news/tsimhoni-children-released-from-juvenile-detention/)

## Parties

- **Dr Maya Eibschitz-Tsimhoni** – mother, ophthalmologist, residing in Bloomfield Hills, Michigan. Israeli-American.
- **Omer Tsimhoni** – father, formerly resident in Michigan; relocated to Israel and remarried following the separation. Israeli-American.
- **Three children**, named in mainstream press coverage as **Liam Tsimhoni** (13–15 across the relevant period), **Roee Tsimhoni** (also spelled "Rowie", 10), and **Natalie Tsimhoni** (also spelled "Nathalie", 9). The children's names appeared in widespread US, Israeli and UK press coverage and in court transcripts that were released publicly during the matter; this page uses the names that appeared in the contemporaneous press, recognising that the children were minors at the time and that some publications later moved to anonymisation.
- **William Lansat** – children's guardian ad litem.
- **Lisa Stern** – attorney for Dr Eibschitz-Tsimhoni at the time of the children's release. (Press records confirm Dr Eibschitz-Tsimhoni used at least 15–16 attorneys across the life of the case; full counsel list **not publicly confirmed**.)

## Background

Dr Eibschitz-Tsimhoni and Mr Tsimhoni divorced in August 2011 after the family had relocated from Israel to Michigan. The father subsequently moved back to Israel and remarried; the children remained with the mother in Bloomfield Hills, a suburb of Detroit ([Patch / Beth Dalbey](https://patch.com/michigan/bloomfield-mi/bloomfield-hills-childrens-mother-persists-fight-remove-judge)).

The post-divorce custody matter was assigned to Oakland County Circuit Judge Lisa Gorcyca and ran for roughly five years and "more than 100 pleadings," including repeated motions invoking parental alienation as a framework for the children's refusal to spend time with their father ([Detroit News](https://www.detroitnews.com/story/news/local/oakland-county/2016/07/01/judge-lisa-gorcyca-guilty-hearing/86609062/)). The mother's case framed the children's resistance as a protective response to alleged paternal violence; the father's case framed it as alienation orchestrated by the mother. Judge Gorcyca repeatedly sided with the father's framing and entered orders intended to compel contact.

## Key facts

- On **24 June 2015**, after the three children refused an order to have lunch with their father at the Oakland County courthouse, Judge Gorcyca held the children in civil contempt and ordered them to **Mandy's Place**, a dormitory wing of the **Children's Village** juvenile facility usually used for abused and neglected children ([Forward / Paul Berger](https://forward.com/news/311751/tshimoni-israeli-detroit-divorce-custody/); [Courthouse News](https://www.courthousenews.com/judge-releases-kids-she-jailed-in-divorce-case/)).
- The children were held there for approximately two weeks.
- On the record at the 24 June hearing, Judge Gorcyca compared the children to the **Charles Manson "Family"** cult, telling the mother (per transcript excerpts reproduced in contemporaneous press): "Your children — you need to do a research program on Charlie Manson and the cult that he has. Your behavior in the hall with me months ago, your behavior in this courtroom, your behavior back there is unlike any I've seen in 46,000 cases." ([Jezebel summary of transcript](https://jezebel.com/judge-jails-kids-compares-them-to-manson-cult-for-refu-1716809001))
- Judge Gorcyca told Liam, the eldest, that he was "supposed to have a high IQ, which I'm doubting right now because of the way you act, you're very defiant, you have no manners" and warned he could remain in detention until age 18 ([Forward / Paul Berger](https://forward.com/news/311751/tshimoni-israeli-detroit-divorce-custody/)).
- After national and international press coverage, the court convened an emergency hearing on or about **9 July 2015** and ordered the children released to a summer camp where both parents could visit ([Innocence Project](https://innocenceproject.org/news/tsimhoni-children-released-from-juvenile-detention/)).
- In **August 2015** the children's principal residence was switched to the father in Israel under a "parental alienation" reunification framework; the mother's contact was sharply curtailed and she reported in 2016 having recovered roughly 68% of her time with the children ([West Michigan Divorce blog](https://westmichigandivorce.com/prevented-from-seeing-their-mother-in-grisly-custody-battle/)).
- Dr Eibschitz-Tsimhoni filed motions for Judge Gorcyca to recuse herself; when refused, she escalated to Oakland County Chief Judge **Nanci Grant** and ultimately Judge Gorcyca was removed from the case ([Patch / Beth Dalbey, 1 Oct 2015 + 6 Oct 2015 articles](https://patch.com/michigan/bloomfield-mi/judge-bitter-custody-dispute-could-recuse-herself)).

## Procedural posture

Three parallel tracks of proceedings:

1. **The underlying custody case** in Oakland County Circuit Court, eventually reassigned away from Judge Gorcyca.
2. **Appellate review:** at least two unpublished opinions of the Michigan Court of Appeals – Docket No. 329406 (2016) and Docket No. 336868 (2018) – addressing aspects of the custody orders ([Justia 2016](https://law.justia.com/cases/michigan/court-of-appeals-unpublished/2016/329406.html); [Justia 2018](https://law.justia.com/cases/michigan/court-of-appeals-unpublished/2018/336868.html)).
3. **Judicial-conduct proceedings:** the Michigan Judicial Tenure Commission filed Formal Complaint No. 98 against Judge Gorcyca arising out of the 24 June 2015 hearing. The Michigan Supreme Court issued public censure in 2017 and lifted a brief suspension that had been imposed by the JTC ([CBS Detroit / WZZM-13 reporting](https://www.cbsnews.com/detroit/news/judge-violated-rules-of-conduct-in-child-custody-case-commission-says/); [WZZM-13](https://www.wzzm13.com/article/news/local/michigan/michigan-high-court-lifts-suspension-on-judge-who-jailed-kids-in-custody-fight/69-460268589)).

## The judgment (and the misconduct finding)

At the 24 June 2015 hearing, Judge Gorcyca purported to act under Michigan's civil-contempt power for the children's refusal to obey her parenting-time order. She characterised the children's behaviour as the product of brainwashing by their mother and described the situation as parental alienation on a scale she had not previously encountered in her caseload.

The Michigan Supreme Court, ruling on the JTC complaint, held that Judge Gorcyca **committed judicial misconduct** by:

- directing "demeaning and disparaging remarks" at the children;
- making false representations in writing to the Commission about her own conduct.

The court ordered **public censure** as proportionate discipline, but declined to suspend her ([Detroit News](https://www.detroitnews.com/story/news/local/oakland-county/2016/07/01/judge-lisa-gorcyca-guilty-hearing/86609062/); [US News](https://www.usnews.com/news/articles/2015-12-15/judge-who-jailed-kids-for-refusing-lunch-with-dad-may-face-removal)).

The Michigan Court of Appeals' 2016 and 2018 unpublished opinions in *Eibschitz-Tsimhoni v Tsimhoni* addressed downstream custody orders. They are unpublished and not precedential, but together they form the appellate trail through which the children's residence and contact arrangements continued to be litigated for years after Judge Gorcyca's removal.

## Reactions and commentary

- The Innocence Project published a brief reflection arguing the case demonstrated how easily children's voices are overridden in the family-court system ([Innocence Project](https://innocenceproject.org/news/tsimhoni-children-released-from-juvenile-detention/)).
- *Forward* (Paul Berger, 9 July 2015) framed the case as the collision of Israeli-American transnational families with US state-court custody machinery ([Forward](https://forward.com/news/311751/tshimoni-israeli-detroit-divorce-custody/)).
- *Haaretz* (Allison Kaplan Sommer, 10 July 2015) gave it the headline "Jailed Michigan Kids Are Victims of Israeli-American Divorce Nightmare" – the article sits behind a paywall but the framing was widely reproduced ([Haaretz](https://www.haaretz.com/.premium-jailed-michigan-kids-are-victims-of-israeli-american-divorce-nightmare-1.5376082)).
- US legal commentary divided sharply along ideological lines. Some men's family-law practices – e.g. [Divorce Lawyers for Men](https://www.divorcelawyersformen.com/blog/tsimhoni-v-tsimhoni-parental-alienation-and-ultimate-consequences/) – framed the case as a parental-alienation success story, citing the eventual transfer of residence to the father. Others, including the Innocence Project and a substantial body of journalism and academic writing on children's procedural rights, treated the 24 June hearing as a textbook abuse of contempt power against children.
- **Canadian family-law commentator Georgialee Lang KC** wrote a multi-part series on the case as a case study in alienation litigation gone wrong, including ["Part 2: Alienated Kids Released From Jail"](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/part-2-alienated-kids-released-from-jail-georgialee-lang-ba-jd-fciarb).
- Domestic-violence and child-advocacy NGOs cited the case in submissions to the US Congressional record around the 2017–2018 hearings on family-court reform; specific submissions **not publicly confirmed** in this summary.

## Why this case matters

Tsimhoni is the US case that most uncomfortably captures everything wrong with the way "parental alienation" can be deployed in family courts. Three children, aged nine to thirteen, were sent to a juvenile detention facility for refusing to have lunch with their father. The judge who put them there compared them to the Manson cult on the record and was later formally found by the Michigan Supreme Court to have engaged in judicial misconduct. And yet the underlying custody framework – switching the children to live with the parent they had been resisting, in the name of breaking alleged alienation – proceeded essentially intact.

For parents trying to understand what "high-conflict" cases can become, Tsimhoni illustrates a recurring pattern: a judge under pressure from a long-running case starts to read the children's resistance as evidence of brainwashing rather than as data to be examined; coercive remedies escalate; and the children's voices are pathologised rather than heard. The fact that one of the named children publicly described the experience years later in interviews adds a rare child-perspective layer rarely available in PA discourse.

It is also a case that complicates the simple "alienation good / alienation bad" framing. The Michigan Supreme Court did not say there was no alienation; it said the judge's *handling* of the alleged alienation – the contempt sanction, the demeaning remarks, the false representations to the JTC – was misconduct. That distinction is the same one Re Y (2026) makes for England and Wales: even if alienation is real in a given case, the remedies have to be lawful, child-respecting, and supported by proper findings. Tsimhoni is the worked example of what happens when they are not.

For US advocates, Tsimhoni is also the rare named, transcribed, appellately-reviewed PA case that escapes the usual PACER-anonymisation problem. It is on the record. It is teachable. And the judicial-conduct ruling provides a hard data point that the methods of forced reunification – contempt against children, juvenile detention, public ridicule from the bench – are not just controversial, they are sanctionable.
