# Cass. civ., sez. I, ord. 24 marzo 2022, n. 9691 ("caso Massaro")

**Court:** Corte Suprema di Cassazione (Italian Court of Cassation), First Civil Section (Sezione Prima Civile), sitting as an ordinanza (ordinance) panel
**Date of decision / udienza camerale:** 24 March 2022
**Date of deposit / pubblicazione:** 24 March 2022 (the date carried on the headnote ordinanza n. 9691/2022)
**Lower courts:** Tribunale per i Minorenni di Roma (first instance, parental-responsibility decree); Corte d'Appello di Roma, sezione per i minorenni (appeal, decree refusing suspension and confirming the child's removal to a casa-famiglia / residential community)
**Applicant / ricorrente:** **Laura Massaro** (mother), identified publicly by herself, her counsel and supporting NGOs; in the body of the ordinanza the parties are anonymised in line with Italian privacy convention for family-court matters

## Parties

- **Ricorrente:** Mrs **Laura Massaro**, Italian national, resident in Rome. She has waived anonymity publicly and her name appears throughout the Italian press and NGO record; the body of the ordinanza itself anonymises the family.
- **Controricorrente / father:** anonymised; his name is **not publicly confirmed** from the judgment text and is therefore not stated here.
- **Minor child:** son, born **not publicly confirmed** (the public record consistently reports he was approximately 12 years old at the time of the Cassation ruling – [Il Post](https://www.ilpost.it/2022/03/25/sindrome-alienazione-parentale-cassazione-laura-massaro/)).
- **Counsel for Mrs Massaro:** lawyers of the **Associazione Differenza Donna** anti-violence centres ([Differenza Donna press release](https://www.differenzadonna.org/news/la-cassazione-da-ragione-a-laura-massaro-ha-vinto-laura-abbiamo-vinto-tutte/)).
- **Panel composition (President, Relatore, Consiglieri):** **not publicly confirmed** in the summaries used here; the names appear on the face of the ordinanza itself, which is publicly available as a PDF via [Studio Ruffinotti](https://www.studioruffinotti.it/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/Cass-civ.-sez.-I-ord.-24-marzo-2022-n.-9691.pdf).

## Background

The Massaro case became, over nine years, the most publicised Italian family-court litigation framed in the language of "alienazione genitoriale" (parental alienation). It began with Laura Massaro's stalking complaint against her former partner shortly after the couple separated. A series of court-appointed forensic experts (consulenti tecnici d'ufficio, CTU) over the following years described the child's reluctance to see his father in terms of "condizionamento materno" / "sindrome di alienazione parentale" (PAS) and recommended interventions to break that supposed conditioning.

On the basis of those expert opinions, the **Tribunale per i Minorenni di Roma** decreed loss of Mrs Massaro's parental responsibility and placement of the child in a residential community (*casa famiglia*), severing day-to-day mother–son contact ([Differenza Donna](https://www.differenzadonna.org/news/la-cassazione-da-ragione-a-laura-massaro-ha-vinto-laura-abbiamo-vinto-tutte/); [Il Post](https://www.ilpost.it/2022/03/25/sindrome-alienazione-parentale-cassazione-laura-massaro/)). The **Corte d'Appello di Roma** refused Mrs Massaro's request to suspend that order, confirming the removal ([Centro Studi Livatino](https://www.centrostudilivatino.it/cassazione-nuovo-stop-allalienazione-parentale/)). She brought the matter to the Court of Cassation.

The case attracted national attention because of Mrs Massaro's public campaign – including a hunger strike outside the courts and a coalition of feminist NGOs led by Differenza Donna and the Di.Re network of anti-violence centres ([Il Post](https://www.ilpost.it/2022/03/25/sindrome-alienazione-parentale-cassazione-laura-massaro/)).

## Key facts

- Mrs Massaro originally reported the father for **stalking** shortly after separation ([Il Post](https://www.ilpost.it/2022/03/25/sindrome-alienazione-parentale-cassazione-laura-massaro/)).
- The family-court proceedings spanned approximately **nine years** to the date of the Cassation ruling ([Il Post](https://www.ilpost.it/2022/03/25/sindrome-alienazione-parentale-cassazione-laura-massaro/)).
- Multiple court-appointed CTU reports described the boy's behaviour using the framework of **PAS / alienazione genitoriale** and recommended structural intervention to break "materna influenza."
- The Tribunale per i Minorenni di Roma decreed **loss of parental responsibility** of the mother and ordered the child to be removed to a *casa famiglia*; this decree was upheld in interim form by the Corte d'Appello di Roma when it refused suspension ([Centro Studi Livatino](https://www.centrostudilivatino.it/cassazione-nuovo-stop-allalienazione-parentale/)).
- The child – then aged about 12 – was **never directly heard by the courts** that made the decree in the form required by Italian and European law ([Differenza Donna](https://www.differenzadonna.org/news/la-cassazione-da-ragione-a-laura-massaro-ha-vinto-laura-abbiamo-vinto-tutte/); [Dirittifondamentali.it](https://dirittifondamentali.it/2022/04/06/cassazione-la-sindrome-da-alienazione-parentale-pas-e-illegittima-cass-civ-sez-i-ord-24-marzo-2022-n-9691/)).
- Mrs Massaro brought a *ricorso per cassazione* against the Corte d'Appello decree.

## Procedural posture

A *ricorso per cassazione* under Italian civil procedure, decided by ordinanza in camera di consiglio of the **First Civil Section** of the Corte Suprema di Cassazione on **24 March 2022**. The First Civil Section is the chamber with subject-matter jurisdiction over family-law and parental-responsibility cassation appeals.

## The decision

The Cassazione *cassò con rinvio* – set aside the decree of the Corte d'Appello di Roma and remanded for fresh consideration ([Centro Studi Livatino](https://www.centrostudilivatino.it/cassazione-nuovo-stop-allalienazione-parentale/); [Dirittifondamentali.it](https://dirittifondamentali.it/2022/04/06/cassazione-la-sindrome-da-alienazione-parentale-pas-e-illegittima-cass-civ-sez-i-ord-24-marzo-2022-n-9691/)).

The First Civil Section held, in operative terms widely reproduced in the Italian press and case-notes:

> "*il richiamo alla sindrome d'alienazione parentale ... non può ritenersi legittimo, costituendo il fondamento pseudoscientifico di provvedimenti gravemente incisivi sulla vita dei minori, in ordine alla perdita della responsabilità genitoriale materna*"
>
> (in English: "the reference to parental alienation syndrome ... cannot be considered legitimate, constituting the pseudoscientific foundation of measures gravely affecting the lives of minors, as regards the loss of maternal parental responsibility")
>
> ([Differenza Donna](https://www.differenzadonna.org/news/la-cassazione-da-ragione-a-laura-massaro-ha-vinto-laura-abbiamo-vinto-tutte/); [Dirittifondamentali.it](https://dirittifondamentali.it/2022/04/06/cassazione-la-sindrome-da-alienazione-parentale-pas-e-illegittima-cass-civ-sez-i-ord-24-marzo-2022-n-9691/))

Three propositions are extracted from the ordinanza by Italian commentators:

1. **PAS / "alienazione genitoriale" cannot be used as the foundation of a child-removal order.** It is a scientifically contested ("pseudoscientific") construct; in the name of joint parenting (bigenitorialità), a court may not strip a child of the maternal figure on the basis of a PAS-style diagnosis without a concrete factual evaluation of harmful conduct by that parent ([Dirittifondamentali.it](https://dirittifondamentali.it/2022/04/06/cassazione-la-sindrome-da-alienazione-parentale-pas-e-illegittima-cass-civ-sez-i-ord-24-marzo-2022-n-9691/); [Giuridica.net](https://giuridica.net/la-sindrome-da-alienazione-parentale-non-basta-per-allontanare-il-minore/)).
2. **The right of the child to be heard (audizione) is mandatory *a pena di nullità*.** A measure as severe as removal to a *casa-famiglia* cannot stand where the child – party in substance to the proceedings – has not been directly heard by the court. The court-appointed expert's interview is **not a substitute** for the judicial hearing of the minor ([Differenza Donna](https://www.differenzadonna.org/news/la-cassazione-da-ragione-a-laura-massaro-ha-vinto-laura-abbiamo-vinto-tutte/)).
3. **Forced coercive removal of children by police force on the basis of PAS-style findings is "*fuori dallo Stato di diritto*"** – outside the rule of law ([Differenza Donna](https://www.differenzadonna.org/news/la-cassazione-da-ragione-a-laura-massaro-ha-vinto-laura-abbiamo-vinto-tutte/); [Centro Studi Livatino](https://www.centrostudilivatino.it/cassazione-nuovo-stop-allalienazione-parentale/)).

The ordinanza expressly built on the First Section's earlier decision in **Cass. civ., sez. I, 17 maggio 2021, n. 13217** (President Genovese, Relatore Caiazzo), which had already held that a judge cannot adopt "*soluzioni prive del necessario conforto scientifico*" (solutions lacking the necessary scientific support) and had criticised the use of "*sindrome della madre malevola*" / PAS frameworks as an inadmissible "*Täterstrafrecht* / *colpa d'autore*" approach – judging the parent on identity rather than on conduct ([Giustizia Insieme – Russo case note, 9 June 2021](https://www.giustiziainsieme.it/it/minori-e-famiglia/1759-la-corte-di-cassazione-sulla-sindrome-di-alienazione-parentale-e-colpa-d-autore-nota-a-corte-di-cassazione-17-maggio-2021-n-13217-pres-genovese-est-caiazzo)).

## Reactions and commentary

- **Differenza Donna** characterised the ordinanza as a vindication not only of Mrs Massaro but of every mother whose contact with her child had been curtailed on PAS grounds: "*Ha vinto Laura, abbiamo vinto tutte*" ("Laura won, we all won") – the title of its 5 April 2022 press release reproducing the full ordinance text ([Differenza Donna](https://www.differenzadonna.org/news/la-cassazione-da-ragione-a-laura-massaro-ha-vinto-laura-abbiamo-vinto-tutte/)).
- The case note in **Dirittifondamentali.it** by the editorial board (6 April 2022) treats 9691/2022 as the operative confirmation of the doctrinal line opened by 13217/2021: PAS is "pseudo-scientific" and may not ground severe interventions ([Dirittifondamentali.it](https://dirittifondamentali.it/2022/04/06/cassazione-la-sindrome-da-alienazione-parentale-pas-e-illegittima-cass-civ-sez-i-ord-24-marzo-2022-n-9691/)).
- **Centro Studi Livatino**, a Catholic legal think-tank, published a parallel commentary noting that the Cassazione had "*posto un nuovo stop all'alienazione parentale*" ([Centro Studi Livatino](https://www.centrostudilivatino.it/cassazione-nuovo-stop-allalienazione-parentale/)).
- The legal portal **Giuridica.net** notes that the doctrine is now consistent in the First Section: PAS alone is not enough to justify removal of a child from the resident parent ([Giuridica.net](https://giuridica.net/la-sindrome-da-alienazione-parentale-non-basta-per-allontanare-il-minore/)).
- The doctrine was reinforced in **Cass. civ., sez. I, ord. 8 febbraio 2024, n. 3576**, where the First Section held: "*non è mai consentito ... trarre dalla diagnosi di una patologia ... una presunzione di colpevolezza o di inadeguatezza al ruolo genitoriale, del tutto sganciata dalla valutazione in fatto dei comportamenti*" – it is never permissible to derive from a diagnostic label a presumption of parental unfitness divorced from the actual facts of conduct ([AIAF case note](https://aiaf-avvocati.it/articolo/1827/cassazione-ordinanza-n-35762024-pubblicata-il-giorno-8-febbraio-2024)).
- **Cass. civ., sez. I, ord. 21 febbraio 2025, n. 4595** further confirmed the doctrine ([avvocaturaefamiglia.com PDF](https://www.avvocaturaefamiglia.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/Ordinanza-Cass.-sez.-I-21-febbraio-2025-n.-4595.pdf)).

## Why this case matters

Cass. 9691/2022 is the Italian counterpart – on the opposite side of the doctrinal coin – to the English decision in *Re Y (Experts and "Alienating Behaviour")* [2026] EWFC 38. *Re Y* warned the English family courts against treating "alienation" as a diagnostic syndrome demanding a particular expert response. *Massaro* / Cass. 9691/2022 went one step further: the Italian Supreme Court held that using PAS framing as the basis of a coercive removal order is *illegitimate* – a *pseudoscientific* foundation that the rule of law cannot support.

For comparative purposes three features stand out:

1. **Symmetry to** ***Re Y***. Both judgments insist that family-court decision-making must be grounded in evidence of behaviour, not in the application of a contested diagnostic label. *Massaro* puts that proposition into the language of constitutional rule-of-law principle ("*fuori dallo Stato di diritto*").
2. **Centrality of the child's voice.** The Cassazione's insistence that audizione del minore is mandatory *a pena di nullità* – and cannot be substituted by the CTU's clinical interview – is the strongest such statement from a European supreme court in the parental-alienation field. It mirrors the procedural-fairness side of *Re Y* (questioning who gets to speak as an "expert") rather than the reasoning side.
3. **A line of authority, not a one-off**. Following 13217/2021, then 9691/2022, then 3576/2024 and 4595/2025, the First Civil Section has built a consolidated doctrine over four years. Comparative lawyers cannot now treat any of these decisions as outliers: they are a consistent jurisprudential current.

For the parental-alienation discourse, *Massaro* is a sobering counterweight to the maximalist position that PA findings should justify whatever orders the court considers necessary. The Italian Supreme Court has said – clearly, and more than once – that this approach is constitutionally inadmissible when it is built on a contested clinical label and not on facts.

For honest PA advocacy, the case is also a model of what robust judicial engagement should look like: it does not deny that one parent can damage a child's relationship with the other; it insists that proof of that damage must come from evidence of conduct, that the child must be heard, and that the remedy must be proportionate.
