# Cassazione civile, sez. I, ord. 21 febbraio 2025, n. 4595 (Ri.El. c. Mo.Ma.)

**Court:** Corte Suprema di Cassazione, Sezione Prima Civile (Italian Supreme Court of Cassation, First Civil Section) — on appeal from Corte d'Appello di Bologna, decree no. 1141/2024 of 21 May 2024, in turn on appeal from Tribunale di Modena

## Parties

- **Ricorrente (appellant in cassation):** "Ri.El." — the mother (anonymised under art. 52 D.Lgs. 196/2003), represented by Avv. Antonio Voltaggio and Avv. Maria Scardia, domiciled in Rome.
- **Controricorrente (respondent):** "Mo.Ma." — the father, represented by Avv. Michela Zanetti.
- **Minori involved:** Mo.Lo. (born 21 January 2007 — turned 18 during the cassation proceedings) and Mo.Ja. (born 5 May 2015, aged ~9 at decision).
- **Court below:** Corte d'Appello di Bologna (decree 1141/2024); Tribunale di Modena at first instance.

## Background

The parents separated by consensual separation homologated on 6 February 2019, with joint custody (affidamento condiviso) of the two sons, the children domiciled with the mother, and a structured paternal access schedule. The father (Mo.Ma.) subsequently filed under art. 709-ter c.p.c. before the Tribunale di Modena, complaining that he was unable to exercise his right of visit. The Tribunale entrusted the children to the social services (affidamento ai servizi sociali) — a limited form of public-services custody — instructed those services to regulate paternal contact and arrange parenting-support interventions, and increased child support.

The mother appealed (reclamo) to the Corte d'Appello di Bologna seeking exclusive custody, suspension of paternal visits, and an increased maintenance contribution. The Court of Appeal rejected the mother's request for exclusive custody. Citing a court-appointed expert report (CTU), it held that the mother had engaged in "un processo progressivo e cronico di denigrazione e delegittimazione della figura paterna" ("a progressive and chronic process of denigration and delegitimisation of the paternal figure") which had induced aversion in the children, particularly Mo.Lo., toward the father. The Court of Appeal confirmed the affidamento ai servizi sociali with assisted visits.

The mother filed for cassation on three grounds, two of which the Supreme Court considered together: (i) failure to directly hear the minors; (ii) uncritical acceptance of a CTU effectively diagnosing parental alienation syndrome (PAS) without considering allegations of domestic violence and witnessed violence.

## Key facts

- The CTU below (per the mother's recourse, at p. 3 of the impugned decree) had asserted that "*il rifiuto verso il padre, emerso solo dopo la separazione, dipende non già da 'un traumatismo interpersonale riconducibile a comportamenti impropri o violenti paterni' ma da un 'processo progressivo e cronico di denigrazione e delegittimazione della figura paterna*'" ("the rejection of the father, having emerged only after the separation, depends not on 'an interpersonal trauma attributable to improper or violent paternal conduct' but on a 'progressive and chronic process of denigration and delegitimisation of the paternal figure'").
- The mother had filed multiple criminal complaints (denunce penali) against the father; all had ended in requests for archiviazione (dismissal). The Corte d'Appello di Bologna treated this as dispositive, writing that the complaints "*non hanno diretta rilevanza in questa sede*" ("have no direct relevance in this venue").
- The CTU described the mother in personological terms — "*presenta un funzionamento personologico che favorisce legami simbiotici… in cui il diverso viene escluso e ostracizzato*" ("she presents a personological functioning that favours symbiotic bonds… in which what is different is excluded and ostracised") — without observation of conduct.
- The elder son Mo.Lo., aged twelve when the proceedings began (2019) and fifteen at appeal, was never heard directly by either trial court; the hearing had been delegated to the consultant. The younger son Mo.Ja. was four when proceedings began.
- The father was alleged to have been aggressive and violent toward both the mother and the children; both children reportedly told the social-service psychologist that they feared the father.

## Procedural posture

Recourse in cassation against the decree of the Corte d'Appello di Bologna no. 1141/2024 (21 May 2024), which had confirmed the Tribunale di Modena's order of affidamento ai servizi sociali with limited paternal visitation. The mother raised three grounds; the Cassation considered the first two jointly and partially accepted them, cassated (quashed) the impugned decree, and remitted the case to the Corte d'Appello di Bologna in different composition for re-examination.

## The judgment

The First Civil Section partially upheld the recourse, cassated the impugned decree, and remitted to a differently-composed panel of the Corte d'Appello di Bologna. The crucial holdings:

**On the uncritical adoption of a personological/PAS-type diagnosis (§§ 11–12).** The Court found the censure "fondata" (well-founded) and held (§ 11.4):

> "*Non è infatti ammissibile far discendere dalla diagnosi di una patologia, anche se scientificamente indiscussa e a maggior ragione se dubbia, una presunzione di colpevolezza o di inadeguatezza al ruolo di genitore, scissa dalla valutazione in fatto dei comportamenti. Nel processo si giudicano i fatti e i comportamenti…*"

("It is not permissible to derive from the diagnosis of a pathology — even if scientifically undisputed, and all the more so if doubtful — a presumption of guilt or of inadequacy in the parental role, divorced from the factual evaluation of conduct. In legal proceedings what is judged is facts and conduct…")

At § 11.3, the Court explicitly aligned itself with — and cited — the prior line of authority:

> "*… il giudice è tenuto ad accertare la veridicità [dei] comportamenti pregiudizievoli per il minore, utilizzando i comuni mezzi di prova, tipici e specifici della materia, incluse le presunzioni, ed a motivare adeguatamente, senza che sia decisivo il giudizio astratto sulla validità o invalidità scientifica della patologia diagnosticata (**Cass. n. 13217 del 17/05/2021; Cass. n. 9691 del 24/03/2022; Cass. n. 3576 dell'08/02/2024**)*."

("…the judge is required to ascertain the truthfulness of [conduct] prejudicial to the minor, using the common means of evidence typical and specific to the matter, including presumptions, and to motivate adequately, without the abstract judgment on the scientific validity or invalidity of the diagnosed pathology being decisive.")

At § 11.2, the Court took the unusual step of noting that "*determinate diagnosi non sono riconosciute dalla maggioranza della comunità scientifica*" ("certain diagnoses are not recognised by the majority of the scientific community"), and referred expressly to the Ministry of Health note of 29 May 2020 responding to parliamentary interrogation no. 4-02405.

**On the failure of the Corte d'Appello to investigate the violence allegations (§§ 13–18).** The Court held the Bologna decree's reasoning — that the criminal complaints "non hanno diretta rilevanza in questa sede" — was "erronee" (erroneous). The mere archiviazione of criminal complaints is not a sufficient ground for the civil judge to exclude that there was unlawful conduct relevant in the civil forum. The Court invoked the Istanbul Convention (art. 3, 26, 31, 32, 48), the ECtHR judgments in *Talpis v Italy* (2 March 2017), *I.M. and others v Italy* (10 November 2022), and *Errico v Italy* (24 February 2009), and warned (§ 17):

> "*meno ancora si deve presumere che ogniqualvolta un genitore ostacoli i contatti tra i figli e l'altro genitore e questi denunci la cd. PAS, lo faccia per occultare le proprie responsabilità in ordine alle violenze agite sul partner.*"

("still less must it be presumed that whenever one parent obstructs contact between the children and the other parent and the other denounces so-called PAS, that they do so to conceal their own responsibility for violence committed against the partner.")

But equally:

> "*… non si può e non si deve consentire che un'allegazione di violenza domestica infondata o peggio strumentale possa essere utilizzata per contrastare il diritto dei figli di mantenere armoniosi ed equilibrati i rapporti con entrambi i genitori…*"

("…it cannot and must not be permitted that an unfounded or, worse, instrumental allegation of domestic violence be used to obstruct the children's right to maintain harmonious and balanced relationships with both parents…")

**On the failure to directly hear the elder child.** The Court confirmed the established principle (citing Cass. 1474/2021, Cass. 12957/2018) that direct judicial hearing and hearing through a consultant are *not equivalent*. The Bologna court's reasoning on this point was deficient. However, because Mo.Lo. had since turned 18, the point was moot for him.

**Principio di diritto enunciated (§ 10)** — concerning the hearing of under-12 minors, codified into a published principle for future cases.

**P.Q.M. (dispositive).** The Court accepted the first two grounds in part, declared the third absorbed, cassated the impugned decree, and remitted to the Corte d'Appello di Bologna in different composition for re-examination, with express obligation to apply the principles set out at paragraphs 10, 12, 12.1, 18, 18.1 and 18.2.

## Reactions and commentary

- **AIAF (Associazione Italiana degli Avvocati per la Famiglia e i Minori)** had already, on the predecessor 3576/2024, published a substantive commentary endorsing the consolidation of the line.
- **dott. Marco Pingitore** — clinical psychologist and family-law commentator — published a detailed analysis on 2 March 2025 on his blog, marcopingitore.it, framing 4595/2025 as confirming that "diagnosi disancorate dai comportamenti" cannot ground a parental-fitness judgment.
- **Studio Legale Mauro** and several specialised practitioners' blogs ("Stop della Cassazione all'alienazione parentale") characterised the decision as part of a now-stabilised triad (9691/2022 → 3576/2024 → 4595/2025) that defines the post-2022 doctrine.
- **APF (Avvocati per le Persone e le Famiglie)** published "L'evoluzione della giurisprudenza di legittimità in tema di alienazione genitoriale: da sindrome a colpa d'autore" tracking the same trajectory.
- **Cittadinanzattiva** and **Differenza Donna** (women's-rights NGOs) have publicly used the line to push back against PA expert findings in lower-court custody proceedings.

## Why this case matters

This is the case the gap demanded: a decision *applying* the 9691/2022 line to overturn a lower court's reliance on a CTU's PA-style diagnosis. Three reasons it matters operationally for the AntiAlienate knowledge corpus:

1. **It cites the pseudoscience line by name and number.** Paragraph 11.3 places Cass. 13217/2021, Cass. 9691/2022 and Cass. 3576/2024 on a single string-cite and treats them as binding doctrine. Future Italian lower courts that uncritically adopt a PA-style CTU now face a clean three-citation reversal target.
2. **It targets the most common failure mode in Italian custody cases.** The Corte d'Appello di Bologna did not openly say "PAS" — it spoke of "denigrazione e delegittimazione della figura paterna" and "funzionamento personologico simbiotico". The Cassation expressly held that this *paraphrase* of PA is also impermissible when it floats free of factual observation. That widens the doctrine beyond the literal "PAS" label.
3. **It binds the personological / pseudoscience holding to the Istanbul Convention violence-screening obligation.** The judgment makes clear that uncritical PA adoption is not a freestanding error — it is an error that systematically *crowds out* the obligation to investigate alleged domestic and witnessed violence. That is the GREVIO observation, now Italian binding precedent.

The case is one of the strongest available primary-source statements anywhere in Europe that PA-style reasoning, regardless of the label used, cannot substitute for fact-finding about conduct.
