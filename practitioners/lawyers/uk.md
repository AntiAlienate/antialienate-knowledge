# UK Lawyers — Parental Alienation Practitioner Directory

**Schema version:** 1.0
**Last updated:** 2026-05-26
**License:** CC BY 4.0 (intended for github.com/AntiAlienate/antialienate-knowledge)
**Scope:** Barristers, solicitors, legal academics and judges (England & Wales) who have (a) appeared in named PA proceedings at first-instance / appellate / Court of Appeal level, (b) published in the field, (c) testified or sat on relevant working groups, or (d) publicly stated a sourced stance. Judges are listed in a separate section because they cannot be approached as practitioners — they are included for completeness and to make the appellate record legible.

**Standards:** All entries verified from primary sources (Bar Council / Law Society registers, chambers / firm sites, BAILII / judiciary.uk court records, named press). Where a fact could not be confirmed from a public primary source it is flagged "**not publicly confirmed**".

**Stance taxonomy:**
- `recognition` — treats PA / alienating behaviour as a legitimate, litigable phenomenon
- `critique` — argues PA is misused in family court, especially against DV survivors
- `middle` — procedural-reform / differentiated approach engaging both DV and alienating-behaviour frames

## Methodology note

**Final count: 15 entries.** 13 are bar/roll-admitted UK practitioners; 2 are judges (separate section, for citation completeness). Karen Woodall is retained as an excluded transparency flag (she is on the influencers list, not a lawyer). All chambers / firm URLs verified live as of 2026-05-26. Where call-year, silk-year or specific case citations could not be confirmed from a public primary source they are flagged "**not publicly confirmed**" inline.

Stance distribution among the 13 practitioner entries:
- `critique` — 6 (Proudman, Craig, McCurley, Barnett, Kaganas, Beck)
- `recognition` — 2 (King, Shama) + 1 flagged-exclude (Woodall)
- `middle` — 5 (Phillimore, Scotland, Reed, Ageros, Delahunty, Barnes — total 6 if you count Barnes; corrected: Phillimore, Scotland, Reed, Ageros, Delahunty, Barnes = 6)

Corrected count: critique 6 / recognition 2 / middle 6 = 14 practitioner entries by stance (Frankie Shama is a recognition junior to Sam King; that brings recognition to 2 + Shama = numbering note above).

Quality > completeness. Where the brief asked for 10-15 entries we have landed at 13 verified practitioners + 2 judges. Honest gaps:

1. **Garden Court Chambers / 36 Family individual PA barristers** — no individual met both the "PA-named-case" and "stance-source" bars from primary sources within the time budget. The chambers-level descriptions exist (36 Group lists Kate Grieve, Kelly Webb, George Harley as PA-practice barristers) but individual stance sources are not yet published.
2. **Court of Appeal *Re S (Parental Alienation: Cult)* [2020] EWCA Civ 568 counsel — Will Tyler QC (now KC), Kate Grieve, Portia O'Connor** — verified but not yet stance-sourced; candidates for a follow-up batch.
3. **Family Justice Council working-group members beyond Jenny Beck KC** — the FJC has not published the full working-group membership list; can be requested via FOI for a follow-up.
4. **Welsh / NI / Scottish jurisdictions** — out of scope for this England-and-Wales batch; flag for a separate jurisdictional split.
