# LATAM Lawyers — Parental Alienation Practitioner Directory

**Schema version:** 1.0
**Last updated:** 2026-05-27
**License:** CC BY 4.0 (intended for github.com/AntiAlienate/antialienate-knowledge)
**Scope:** Latin-American family-law practitioners (advogados / abogados / abogadas) in **Brazil, Mexico, Argentina, Colombia, Chile** who have (a) appeared as named counsel / promovente in apex PA litigation (STJ, SCJN, CSJN, CSJ Colombia / Corte Constitucional, Corte Suprema Chile), (b) published in the field at scholarly / book-length level, (c) chaired an official PA-related expert body, or (d) publicly stated a sourced stance through institutional advocacy. Institutional entries (feminist legal collectives, IBDFAM, ASAPMI) are listed where the institution itself has filed a court action / issued a sourced PA position and individual named members are confirmable.

**Standards:** All entries verified from primary sources — national bar / firm / institutional website, official court records (STJ, STF, SCJN, CSJN, SCBA, Corte Constitucional de Colombia, Corte Suprema de Chile), peer-reviewed publication, or named press. Where a fact cannot be confirmed from a public primary source it is flagged "**not publicly confirmed**".

**Stance taxonomy:**
- `recognition` — treats parental alienation / alienação parental / alienación parental as a legitimate, litigable phenomenon
- `critique` — argues PA / SAP is misused in family courts, especially against domestic-violence survivor mothers; supports statutory repeal (Lei 12.318/2010) or proscription
- `middle` — procedural-reform / differentiated approach engaging both DV and alienating-behaviour frames; supports keeping framework while disciplining application
- `not_publicly_confirmed` — included for record-completeness; PA-specific stance not in primary sources

**Cross-references:** Anchor case studies are in `/case-studies/` — Brazilian STJ trilogy (REsp 1.859.228/SP Andrighi 2024; REsp 2.108.750/GO 2024); Mexican SCJN Acción de Inconstitucionalidad 11/2016 Oaxaca; Argentine CSJN P.B., E.G. c/ B., K.E. (7-X-2021) + SCBA C.121.539 (9-VIII-2024). Therapists/psychologists (Mizrahi pairs with Pedro Herscovici as a psychologist) are excluded from this lawyer-only file.

## Honest gaps

1. **Apex-court counsel anonymisation.** Brazilian (STJ), Argentine (CSJN, SCBA) and Colombian (Corte Constitucional T-526/23 tutela) family-court privacy conventions anonymise child parties and frequently their counsel. Named-counsel entries are therefore concentrated on (a) constitutional-action promoventes where standing is institutional (DDHPO Oaxaca; CJEF Mexico; AAIG/Themis Brazil; Sisma Mujer Colombia) and (b) doctrinal-commentary authors (Mizrahi). True named-counsel-of-record entries for the STJ 1.859.228 / 2.108.750, CSJN 7-X-2021, and Corte Const. T-526/23 cases would require institutional expediente retrieval beyond public-source search.
2. **Chile sparsity.** Chile lacks a single anchor apex-court PA ruling comparable to Mexico 11/2016, Colombia T-526/23, or Argentina P.B., E.G. The 2023 SAP-as-violence legislative proposal was the most recent significant flashpoint; both flagship entries (Andrea Muñoz at the Supreme Court level and Corporación Humanas at the civil-society level) are partial-fit rather than perfect-fit.
3. **PUC Chile / Centro UC de la Familia.** Carmen Domínguez Hidalgo's academic record is the most extensive Chilean family-law output in the region but a direct PA-specific publication was not surfaced in available sources. A real Spanish-language academic-library pass (Westlaw / vLex Chile, Centro UC de la Familia *Estudios de Derecho de Familia* I-IV table-of-contents) would likely identify a PA piece and convert her from excluded to Tier 2.
4. **Critique-camp named-counsel verification.** The Argentine "feminist-coalition lawyer who supplied Alsalem with Brazil critique" cannot be cleanly disaggregated from the broader AAIG/Themis/Scarance Fernandes/Recchia + Brazilian congressional architecture; the brief's hypothesis that a single named Brazilian advocate fronted the Alsalem submission appears to overstate institutional reality — UN Alsalem's call drew on the AAIG-Themis-coalition collective work and the Câmara dos Deputados feminist deputies' bills (Melchionna, Bomfim, Reis on PL 2812/22), not on a single named advocate.
5. **Mexico CDMX private practice.** Mexico's PA jurisprudence is dominated by institutional / constitutional-action lawyers (Peimbert Calvo, Ríos González); private-practice family lawyers handling individual SCJN amparo cases in CDMX are not retrievable from public-source searches at directory level. A real OAB/Barra Mexicana-de-Abogados register query would be required.
