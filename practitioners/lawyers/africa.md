**Schema version:** 1.0
**Last updated:** 2026-05-27
**License:** CC BY 4.0 (intended for github.com/AntiAlienate/antialienate-knowledge)
**Scope:** Africa-based family-law practitioners (attorneys / advocates / senior counsel) and PA-relevant institutional bodies in **South Africa (anchor jurisdiction), Kenya, Nigeria, Ghana, Tanzania, Zimbabwe, Egypt**. Inclusion gated on at least one of: (a) named counsel / curator ad litem of record in apex or reported PA / international-abduction litigation, (b) peer-reviewed or book-length publication in the field, (c) institutional thought-leadership with PA-relevant publication portfolio, (d) named press as PA / Hague family-law expert.

**Standards:** All entries verified from primary sources — Pretoria Bar / Johannesburg Society of Advocates / Law Society of South Africa / firm or NGO website / official court records (SAFLII, Kenya Law, Law Library SA) / peer-reviewed journal. Where a fact cannot be confirmed from a public primary source it is flagged "**not publicly confirmed**".

**Stance taxonomy:**
- `recognition` — treats parental alienation as a litigable harm to the child and to the rejected parent; supports curator ad litem / forensic-clinical / coercive contact orders
- `critique` — argues PA is misused in family courts (especially against DV-survivor mothers); supports proscription or strong DV carve-outs
- `middle` — procedural-reform / differentiated approach: investigates alienating behaviours but pairs with DV-protection and child-voice safeguards
- `not_publicly_confirmed` — included for record-completeness; PA-specific stance not in primary sources

## South Africa
