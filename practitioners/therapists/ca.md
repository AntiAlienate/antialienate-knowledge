# Canada Therapists & Clinicians — Parental Alienation Practitioner Directory

**Schema version:** 1.0
**Last updated:** 2026-05-26
**License:** CC BY 4.0 (intended for github.com/AntiAlienate/antialienate-knowledge)
**Scope:** Canada-based registered psychologists, psychiatrists, registered social workers, registered psychotherapists, family mediators, parenting coordinators and PA-relevant academics who have (a) operated or directed PA-specific interventions / reunification programmes, (b) given expert-witness evidence in Canadian family-court PA cases, (c) published peer-reviewed work on PA / alienating behaviour / domestic-abuse overlap, or (d) sat on government, AFCC or Family-Justice working groups shaping Canadian PA practice. Stance is sourced, not inferred.

**Standards:** All entries verified from primary sources (provincial regulatory college register where searchable, practice/institutional website, CanLII reported judgments, peer-reviewed publications, named press). Where a fact could not be confirmed from a public primary source it is flagged "**not publicly confirmed**".

**Stance taxonomy:**
- `recognition` — treats parental alienation as a real, assessable phenomenon; operates or endorses PA-specific reunification / "alienating-behaviour" expert-witness work
- `critique` — argues the PA construct is operationalised against domestic-abuse survivors (especially mothers); advocates DV/trauma-informed alternatives and tighter regulation
- `middle` — engages both alienating-behaviour and family-violence frames; works within AFCC-aligned differential-diagnosis approach (Fidler/Bala/Saini *Children Who Resist* model)

**Critical Canadian regulatory & policy context (must read before using this directory):**
- The federal **Divorce Act** (as amended by Bill C-78, in force 1 Mar 2021) requires courts to consider "family violence" as part of the best-interests-of-the-child test (s. 16(3)(j)) and explicitly directs that an evaluation of the impact of family violence on a parent's ability to care for the child be conducted (s. 16(4)). This is the federal anchor for both camps. See https://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/d-3.4/ .
- In Quebec, the **National Assembly's Committee on Institutions hearings** (2021–2023) and the **Rebâtir la confiance** report directly addressed how "syndrome d'aliénation parentale" has been weaponised against DV survivors; see Lapierre (2020) and Zaccour (2020) below.
- The **House of Commons Standing Committee on the Status of Women (FEWO)** received a 2024 NAWL (National Association of Women and the Law) brief calling to "ban parental alienation accusations in family court" — https://nawl.ca/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/NAWL-FEWO-brief-PA-EN.pdf . The brief draws heavily on the academics listed in the critique camp below.
- There is no Canadian equivalent of England's *Re Y [2026] EWFC 38* judgment formally restricting unregulated PA experts; therefore the Canadian directory does **not** carry an explicit "regulatory exclusion" section. Standards-of-evidence concerns are noted at entry level instead.
