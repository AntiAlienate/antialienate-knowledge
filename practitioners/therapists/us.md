# US Therapists & Clinicians — Parental Alienation Practitioner Directory

**Schema version:** 1.0
**Last updated:** 2026-05-26
**License:** CC BY 4.0 (intended for github.com/AntiAlienate/antialienate-knowledge)
**Scope:** US-licensed (or formerly licensed) psychologists, psychiatrists, LMFTs, and clinical social workers who have (a) operated PA-specific intervention programs (reunification camps, intensives, OBFC), (b) published peer-reviewed work on PA or trauma-informed alternatives, or (c) testified/consulted as expert witnesses in PA-related custody litigation. Stance is sourced, not inferred.

**Standards:** All entries verified from primary sources (practice/institutional websites, state board records, peer-reviewed publications, named court records, named press coverage). Where a fact could not be confirmed it is flagged "**not publicly confirmed**".

**Stance taxonomy:**
- `recognition` — treats PA as a diagnosable phenomenon; operates or endorses PA-specific interventions / reunification
- `critique` — argues PA construct is misused in family court, especially against DV survivors; advocates trauma-informed alternatives
- `middle` — engages both family-violence and alienating-behaviour frames; AFCC-aligned, differential-diagnosis / RRD / PCCP approach
