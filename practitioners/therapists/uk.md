# UK Therapists & Clinicians — Parental Alienation Practitioner Directory

**Schema version:** 1.0
**Last updated:** 2026-05-26
**License:** CC BY 4.0 (intended for github.com/AntiAlienate/antialienate-knowledge)
**Scope:** UK-based (England, Wales, Scotland, Northern Ireland) psychologists, psychiatrists, psychotherapists, clinical social workers and PA-specialist academics who have (a) operated or directed PA-specific interventions / "alienating behaviour" assessment practices, (b) given expert-witness evidence in UK family-court PA cases, (c) published peer-reviewed work on PA / alienating behaviour / domestic-abuse overlap, or (d) sat on the BPS / Family Justice Council / Cafcass working groups shaping UK PA guidance. Stance is sourced, not inferred.

**Standards:** All entries verified from primary sources (HCPC register, BPS publication record, practice/institutional websites, reported UK judgments, named press coverage). Where a fact could not be confirmed it is flagged "**not publicly confirmed**".

**Stance taxonomy:**
- `recognition` — treats parental alienation as a real and assessable phenomenon; operates or endorses PA-specific intervention / "alienating-behaviour" expert-witness work
- `critique` — argues the PA construct is misused in family court, especially against domestic-abuse survivors; advocates trauma-informed alternatives and tighter regulation of "PA experts"
- `middle` — engages both alienating-behaviour and family-violence frames; works within FJC / BPS / AFCC-aligned differential-diagnosis approach

**Critical UK regulatory context (must read before using this directory):**
- In **Re Y (Experts and Alienating Behaviour: The Modern Approach) [2026] EWFC 38** (20 Feb 2026), Sir Andrew McFarlane P set aside findings of "alienation" made against a domestic-abuse-survivor mother. The judgment held that the unregulated expert Melanie Gill — "neither a chartered psychologist, nor registered with the Health and Care Professions Council" — should not have been instructed, and that going forward "permission should not be given for the instruction of an expert 'psychologist' who is neither registered by a relevant statutory body, nor chartered by the BPS." See https://www.judiciary.uk/courts-and-tribunals/family-court/ and reporting at https://www.thebureauinvestigates.com/stories/2026-02-27/dont-use-unregulated-psychologists-top-judge-rules .
- The **Family Justice Council Guidance on Alienating Behaviours (December 2024)** formally records that "'parental alienation syndrome' has no evidential basis and is considered a harmful pseudo-science" (para 2) while accepting that Alienating Behaviours (AB) as a behavioural pattern can be a fact for the court to find. Source: https://www.judiciary.uk/wp-content/uploads/2024/12/Family-Justice-Council-Guidance-on-responding-to-allegations-of-alienating-behaviour-2024-1-1.pdf .
- The **BPS / FJC 2022 Guidance on Psychologists as Expert Witnesses in the Family Courts** sets the regulatory floor: https://www.bps.org.uk/guideline/psychologists-expert-witnesses-family-courts-england-wales .

**Excluded practitioners (regulatory disclosure):**
- **Melanie Gill** — unregulated; not HCPC-registered; not chartered by BPS; no clinical/therapeutic practice. Findings against the mother in the Re Y case were set aside by McFarlane P in Feb 2026 with explicit reasons. Reporting: https://www.thebureauinvestigates.com/stories/2026-02-13/family-courts-gill-vindicated . **Excluded** as practising clinician — direct UK parallel to how Randy Rand is excluded in the US therapists list.
