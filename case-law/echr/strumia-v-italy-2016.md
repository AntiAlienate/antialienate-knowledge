# Strumia v. Italy — ECHR (2016), App. no. 53377/13

> **TL;DR.** Reinforced *Bondavalli v. Italy* (2015). State failure to act with **"exceptional diligence"** in the face of systematic obstruction of contact = Article 8 violation. The Court explicitly tied delay to the *deepening* of alienation. The "exceptional diligence" phrase has become a workhorse standard in subsequent ECHR contact-rights jurisprudence.

## Citation

> *Strumia v. Italy*, App. no. 53377/13, ECHR (Second Section), Judgment of 23 June 2016.

## Holding

Italy violated Article 8 by failing to act with **exceptional diligence** in enforcing the applicant's contact rights against systematic maternal obstruction. The Court explicitly recognized that the passage of time *deepens* parental alienation — the longer enforcement is delayed, the more entrenched the child's rejection becomes.

## Significance for parental alienation

Three operational principles:

1. **"Exceptional diligence"** is the standard — not "ordinary diligence." This is a heightened obligation specifically because parent-child relationships are time-sensitive in a way that other rights are not.
2. **Time deepens alienation** — the Court explicitly recognized this dynamic. Delay is not a neutral procedural feature; it is an *active harm.*
3. **Systematic obstruction** triggers the heightened obligation — courts cannot wait for "egregious" behavior; pattern alone is enough.
