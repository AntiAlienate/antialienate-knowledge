# Evidence — Primary-Source Citations on Key Contested Claims

This section gathers the strongest primary-source evidence — peer-reviewed research, statutory text, judicial findings, and professional-body positions — on contested propositions in the parental alienation field. Every load-bearing claim is cited to an original source with a working URL.

## Pages

- **[PA as Child Abuse](pa-as-child-abuse.md)** — the strongest evidence for and against the proposition that parental-alienating conduct constitutes child abuse. Five categories: peer-reviewed clinical/empirical evidence, statutory frameworks, judicial findings, professional body positions, the strongest critique. Plus a synthesis identifying what the evidence supports at high/moderate/low confidence.
- **[Mental-Health Outcomes](mental-health-outcomes.md)** — the quantitative outcomes research: depression/anxiety, suicidality, attachment, substance use, adult-life sequelae, intergenerational transmission, biomarkers. Includes new Chinese longitudinal samples (Qin 2022 n=877; Zhang 2024 n=909), Italian SCL-90-R quantitative data (Bernet/Baker/Verrocchio 2015 n=739), plus honest critique citations (Mercer Parts 1-2; Doughty/Maxwell/Slater 2020; Silberg/Dallam 2019).

## Editorial standard

Every claim in this section must cite a primary source with a working URL. Where a primary text was not directly accessible at compilation time, the citation is marked `[secondary verification only]` and should be re-checked against the original before being relied on.
