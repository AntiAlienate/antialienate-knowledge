# Contributing to AntiAlienate Knowledge Base

We accept PRs from targeted parents, family-law attorneys, clinicians, researchers, judges, journalists, and anyone working in the field of parental alienation.

## Ground rules

1. **Verifiable or it doesn't ship.** Every claim needs a primary source. No fabrication, no padding.
2. **Factual neutrality.** This is a reference work, not advocacy. Frame everything in evidence-based, professionally-toned language.
3. **No personal attacks.** Behavioral and institutional patterns can be documented. Specific individuals (other than published expert authors) cannot be named in adverse contexts without explicit consent and compliance-lawyer review.
4. **Disclaimer is mandatory.** Templates and frameworks include the standard disclaimer: "Educational content. Not legal advice. Consult licensed family-law counsel in your jurisdiction."
5. **License compliance.** New content must be compatible with CC BY 4.0 (content) or CC0 (data). Do not submit copyrighted material you don't own or aren't licensing under these terms.

## What we accept

| Type | Required source |
|---|---|
| **Research summary** | DOI link to peer-reviewed paper OR established academic repository |
| **Case-law entry** | Link to official court ruling (court website, ECHR HUDOC, established legal database) |
| **Statute** | Link to official government source for the code/article |
| **Template** | Disclaimer, jurisdictional notes, last-reviewed date |
| **Translation** | Human review confirmation + native speaker attribution |
| **Infographic** | SVG (vector, editable), with source list embedded as metadata |
| **Story** (community/) | Anonymized, consent confirmed, no third-party identifying details |

## What we don't accept

- Anecdotal claims without sources
- AI-generated content without human review and verification
- Defamatory or personally-targeted material
- Content that violates the licenses of its source
- Conspiratorial framing (e.g., "the family court system is rigged" without sourced evidence)
- Junk-science framing (e.g., claims that contradict peer-reviewed consensus without acknowledging mainstream critique)

## How to contribute

1. **Fork** this repository
2. **Create a branch** named for your contribution (e.g., `add-belgium-cour-de-cassation-2024-ruling`)
3. **Add or edit content** following the structure of existing files
4. **Run a sanity check**: do your sources resolve, is the citation format consistent, is the disclaimer present where required?
5. **Open a Pull Request** with:
   - What you added/changed
   - Sources cited
   - Jurisdiction (if applicable)
   - Your name and qualifications (so we can attribute correctly)

## Disclosures

Contributors who are:
- **Family-law attorneys** should disclose firm affiliation
- **Clinicians/researchers** should disclose institutional affiliation
- **Targeted parents** can contribute anonymously or under a pseudonym if preferred

## Attribution

All contributors are credited in [CONTRIBUTORS.md](./CONTRIBUTORS.md) (created on first merge).

## Code of conduct

Be useful. Be sourced. Be factual. The children come first. Disagreements about interpretation are welcome; personal attacks are not.
