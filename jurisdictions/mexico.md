# Mexico — Parental Alienation Legal Reference

> Jurisdiction-specific legal reference for parents, lawyers, and clinicians navigating parental-alienation matters in Mexico. Cross-referenced with deeply-investigated case studies. CC BY 4.0.

## Quick reference

| Element | Mexico position |
|---|---|
| Statutory PA recognition | Patchwork at the state-civil-code level; SCJN has struck down the broadest formulations as unconstitutionally vague. There is no federal civil-code PA provision (family law is reserved to the states under art. 124 CPEUM). |
| Apex court direction (current) | Sceptical of vague PA definitions and of automatic patria-potestad consequences. [SCJN, Acción de Inconstitucionalidad 11/2016](../case-studies/scjn-accion-inconstitucionalidad-11-2016-alienacion-parental-oaxaca.md) struck down the Oaxaca civil-code formulation. |
| Standard of proof for PA findings | Civil standard (*sana crítica*) under state codes of civil procedure; *interés superior del menor* under art. 4º CPEUM and the *Ley General de los Derechos de Niñas, Niños y Adolescentes (LGDNNA)*. |
| Forensic-evaluator framework | Court-appointed *perito psicólogo* per state codes; *equipo multidisciplinario* of the *Juzgado Familiar*; *DIF* (Sistema para el Desarrollo Integral de la Familia) participation. |
| Reunification programs court-ordered | Limited and state-variable; *convivencias supervisadas* in *Centros de Convivencia Familiar* (CCF); coercive residential reunification is not standard. |
| Domestic-abuse counterweight in place | Yes. *Ley General de Acceso de las Mujeres a una Vida Libre de Violencia (LGAMVLV)*; *órdenes de protección*; *Convención de Belém do Pará*. |

## Statutory framework

- **Constitución Política de los Estados Unidos Mexicanos (CPEUM), art. 4º** — *interés superior de la niñez*; right to family life. [DOF — CPEUM](https://www.diputados.gob.mx/LeyesBiblio/pdf/CPEUM.pdf).
- **Constitución Política de los Estados Unidos Mexicanos (CPEUM), art. 124** — reservation of residual powers (including family law) to the states.
- **Ley General de los Derechos de Niñas, Niños y Adolescentes (LGDNNA), 2014** — federal floor of children's rights, including *interés superior del menor* and right to be heard. [DOF / Diputados](https://www.diputados.gob.mx/LeyesBiblio/pdf/LGDNNA.pdf).
- **Ley General de Acceso de las Mujeres a una Vida Libre de Violencia (LGAMVLV), 2007** — federal DV framework; *órdenes de protección*. [DOF / Diputados](https://www.diputados.gob.mx/LeyesBiblio/pdf/LGAMVLV.pdf).
- **State civil and family codes** — substantive family law, including *patria potestad*, *guarda y custodia*, *convivencias*. The 32 state codes vary widely on whether and how *alienación parental* is referenced.
- **Código Nacional de Procedimientos Civiles y Familiares (CNPCyF), 2023** — unified national procedural code progressively replacing state codes of civil procedure. [DOF — CNPCyF](https://www.diputados.gob.mx/LeyesBiblio/pdf/CNPCF.pdf).

## Apex court direction

The leading constitutional-level reference is **[Acción de Inconstitucionalidad 11/2016 (SCJN — Pleno, 23–26 October 2017, engrose published in DOF 16 May 2018)](../case-studies/scjn-accion-inconstitucionalidad-11-2016-alienacion-parental-oaxaca.md)**:

- The SCJN's Pleno (ponente Ministra Norma Lucía Piña Hernández) examined Decreto 1380 of Oaxaca, which had amended the state Civil Code to define *alienación parental* as conduct that "transforms the consciousness" of a child to impair the bond with the other parent (art. 336 Bis B), to classify it as a species of *violencia familiar*, and to attach automatic suspension or loss of *patria potestad* (art. 429 Bis A).
- The Pleno declared the broadest formulations unconstitutional on grounds of vagueness, disproportionality, and incompatibility with the *interés superior del menor* and the principle of *taxatividad* (*lex certa*) in provisions producing severe consequences.
- The decision did not declare every state-level PA provision unconstitutional, but established the parameters within which state legislatures may formulate such provisions: definitions must be sufficiently precise, consequences must be proportionate and individualised, and *patria potestad* may not be suspended automatically without case-specific evaluation.

State-level *Tribunales Superiores de Justicia* have, post-AI 11/2016, generally moved toward neutral-language formulations focused on conduct (*conducta obstruccionista de la convivencia*) rather than syndromic labels.

## Standard of proof

Mexican family-court procedure operates under *sana crítica* / *libre valoración* of evidence. The substantive standard for any order affecting *patria potestad* or *guarda* is *interés superior del menor* per art. 4º CPEUM, LGDNNA, and the SCJN's *Primera Sala* jurisprudence (*tesis de jurisprudencia* on the *interés superior*).

## Forensic evaluation framework

- **Perito psicólogo / perito en trabajo social** — appointed per state codes of civil procedure (progressively unified under the CNPCyF).
- **Equipo multidisciplinario** of the *Juzgado Familiar* — psicología + trabajo social attached to family courts in larger jurisdictions.
- **Sistema para el Desarrollo Integral de la Familia (DIF)** — federal and state DIF agencies provide social-work investigation and *convivencias supervisadas* infrastructure.
- **Procuraduría de Protección de Niñas, Niños y Adolescentes** under LGDNNA — child-protection authority with standing in family-court proceedings.
- Post-AI 11/2016, perito evidence invoking PAS as a syndromic category faces elevated *amparo* exposure if used to ground severe consequences.

## Reunification programs

- **Centros de Convivencia Familiar (CCF)** — state-operated supervised-contact infrastructure; capacity and quality vary significantly by state (CDMX, Estado de México, Nuevo León, Jalisco have the largest networks).
- **Convivencias asistidas / supervisadas** — court-ordered supervised visits typically held at CCF.
- **Mediación familiar** — *Centros de Justicia Alternativa* attached to state judiciaries.
- US/Anglo-style coercive residential reunification camps are not part of the Mexican toolkit.

## Domestic-violence interaction

- **LGAMVLV** sets federal DV framework; *órdenes de protección* are emergency civil measures.
- **Ley General de Víctimas (2013)** and the *Sistema Nacional de Atención a Víctimas* provide victim-support infrastructure.
- **Convención de Belém do Pará** ratified by Mexico, with CEVI follow-up; CEDAW Concluding Observations on Mexico have repeatedly addressed the family-court treatment of DV.
- The SCJN's *Protocolo para juzgar con perspectiva de género* (latest edition) directs judges to evaluate DV allegations independently and to avoid framings that minimise gender violence.

## Honest gaps

- A consolidated state-by-state survey of *alienación parental* civil-code provisions post-AI 11/2016 is **not publicly confirmed** in a single citable source.
- The CNPCyF's gradual displacement of state procedural codes is uneven; this reference does not map state-by-state implementation.
- SCJN *Primera Sala* PA-adjacent *amparo directo* jurisprudence has produced a body of *tesis aisladas* and *jurisprudencia por contradicción* that is not surveyed here.
