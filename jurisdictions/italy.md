# Italy — Parental Alienation Legal Reference

> Jurisdiction-specific legal reference for parents, lawyers, and clinicians navigating parental-alienation matters in Italy. Cross-referenced with deeply-investigated case studies. CC BY 4.0.

## Quick reference

| Element | Italy position |
|---|---|
| Statutory PA recognition | No. The Codice civile (artt. 337-bis ff., post-2006 reform) does not use the term *alienazione parentale*. The framework operates through *affidamento condiviso* (joint custody), *affidamento ai servizi sociali*, and the residual *affidamento super-esclusivo*. |
| Apex court direction (current) | Sceptical/in-retreat. The Corte di Cassazione has explicitly characterised PAS as scientifically unfounded in [Cass. civ. sez. I, ord. n. 9691/2022 ("caso Massaro")](../case-studies/cass-civ-sez-i-ord-9691-2022-massaro-alienazione-parentale.md) and reiterated this in [Cass. civ. sez. I, ord. n. 4595/2025](../case-studies/cass-civ-sez-i-ord-4595-2025-corte-appello-bologna-alienazione-parentale.md). |
| Standard of proof for PA findings | Civil standard (*libero convincimento del giudice*) under art. 116 c.p.c.; *interesse superiore del minore* governs. |
| Forensic-evaluator framework | *Consulente tecnico d'ufficio (CTU)* under arts. 61–64 c.p.c.; mandatory child-hearing under art. 315-bis c.c. and art. 336-bis c.c.; *curatore speciale* under the *Riforma Cartabia*. |
| Reunification programs court-ordered | *Affidamento ai servizi sociali* with assisted visits; placement in *casa-famiglia* in extreme cases (now post-Massaro under elevated ECHR/Cassation scrutiny). |
| Domestic-abuse counterweight in place | Yes. *Codice rosso* (Legge 69/2019); *Convenzione di Istanbul*; *GREVIO 2020 Italy report*; ECHR cluster (Lombardo, Strumia, Improta) on art. 8 ECHR procedural duties. |

## Statutory framework

- **Codice civile, artt. 315-bis – 337-octies** — post-2006 / post-2013 / post-Cartabia parental-responsibility framework. [Normattiva — Codice civile](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:codice.civile).
- **Codice civile, art. 337-quater** — *affidamento esclusivo* (exclusive custody) as exception to the *affidamento condiviso* default. [Normattiva](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:codice.civile:art.337-quater).
- **Codice civile, art. 333** — court powers in cases of *condotta pregiudizievole* (prejudicial conduct) of a parent. [Normattiva](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:codice.civile:art.333).
- **Codice di procedura civile, art. 709-ter** — sanctions and remedies for non-compliance with parenting orders. [Normattiva](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:codice.procedura.civile:art.709-ter).
- **Codice di procedura civile, arts. 61–64** — CTU framework. [Normattiva](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:codice.procedura.civile).
- **Riforma Cartabia (Decreto legislativo 10 ottobre 2022, n. 149)** — major procedural reform of family-court litigation, including new rules on CTU, *curatore speciale*, and child-hearing. [Normattiva](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:decreto.legislativo:2022-10-10;149).
- **Legge 19 luglio 2019, n. 69 ("Codice Rosso")** — accelerated criminal procedure for DV offences. [Normattiva](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:legge:2019-07-19;69).
- **Convenzione di Istanbul** ratified by Italy via Legge 27 giugno 2013, n. 77.

## Apex court direction

Italy has produced one of the most pointed apex-court rejections of PAS as a clinical category in Europe:

- **[Cass. civ. sez. I, ord. 24 marzo 2022, n. 9691 ("caso Massaro")](../case-studies/cass-civ-sez-i-ord-9691-2022-massaro-alienazione-parentale.md)** — the Corte di Cassazione set aside lower-court decrees that had transferred the child of Laura Massaro to a *casa-famiglia* (residential community) on a CTU-driven PAS / "*condizionamento materno*" finding. The Court characterised PAS as a clinical construct lacking scientific support and held that lower courts may not premise the most invasive interventions on such a construct.

- **[Cass. civ. sez. I, ord. 21 febbraio 2025, n. 4595](../case-studies/cass-civ-sez-i-ord-4595-2025-corte-appello-bologna-alienazione-parentale.md)** — reiterated and extended the Massaro line in the context of an *affidamento ai servizi sociali* order from the Corte d'Appello di Bologna; the panel (Presidente Acierno; Relatrice Russo) emphasised that uncritical acceptance of a CTU report invoking parental alienation, without consideration of DV allegations, violates the procedural rights of the mother and the child.

- **ECHR cluster — [Lombardo v. Italy (no. 25704/11, 29 January 2013)](../case-studies/lombardo-v-italy-echr-25704-11-2013.md); [Strumia v. Italy (no. 53377/13, 23 June 2016)](../case-studies/strumia-v-italy-echr-53377-13-2016.md); [Improta v. Italy (no. 66396/14, 4 May 2017)](../case-studies/improta-v-italy-echr-66396-14-2017.md)** — Strasbourg has repeatedly found Italy in breach of art. 8 ECHR for failure to take appropriate measures to enforce contact orders and to protect the parent-child relationship from passing of time. These judgments cut in both directions and bind Italian family-court practice on procedural diligence.

## Standard of proof

Italian civil procedure operates on the *libero convincimento del giudice* (judge's free conviction) under art. 116 c.p.c., reasoned according to *prudente apprezzamento*. There is no formal "clear and convincing" tier. *Interesse superiore del minore* under art. 315-bis c.c. and CRC art. 3 governs all child-welfare reasoning.

## Forensic evaluation framework

- **CTU (consulente tecnico d'ufficio)** under arts. 61–64 c.p.c. — court-appointed expert in contested family-court matters. Post-Riforma Cartabia, CTU operates under tightened methodological and procedural rules.
- **Curatore speciale del minore** — strengthened by the Riforma Cartabia; mandatory in defined categories of conflict.
- **Ascolto del minore** under art. 315-bis c.c. and art. 336-bis c.c. — mandatory hearing of the child where the child is capable of discernment; methodological protocols apply.
- Post-Massaro / 4595/2025, Italian family courts that adopt a CTU PAS framing without independent methodological scrutiny face elevated reversal risk.

## Reunification programs

- **Affidamento ai servizi sociali** — entrusting the child to the social services (typically while continuing residence with one parent) for purposes of monitoring contact and providing parenting-support interventions.
- **Casa-famiglia placement** — residential community placement; the Massaro decision and post-Massaro Cassation line have placed this remedy under heightened scrutiny.
- **Visite protette / vigilate** — supervised or assisted visits, often through *Spazi neutri* / *Servizi sociali*.
- US/Anglo-style coercive residential reunification camps are not part of the Italian toolkit.

## Domestic-violence interaction

- **Codice rosso (L. 69/2019)** — accelerated criminal procedure and protective measures.
- **Convenzione di Istanbul** — ratified, with substantial GREVIO follow-up.
- The **GREVIO 2020 Italy baseline report** flagged Italian family-court use of PAS framings as a serious concern under the Convention.
- Post-Massaro / 4595/2025, Italian Cassation has explicitly held that DV allegations must be evaluated before any PA / alienation framing can support an invasive intervention; uncritical adoption of a CTU report that bypasses the DV evidence is a ground for annulment.

## Honest gaps

- The full text of the post-Cartabia ministerial circulars on CTU methodology is not consolidated in a single citable URL; practitioner commentary is the primary source.
- The downstream effect of Massaro / 4595/2025 on Tribunali per i Minorenni practice is documented unevenly across regions.
- Italian academic literature on PAS produced by *Centro Studi Livatino* and *Differenza Donna* takes diametrically opposed positions; this reference does not adjudicate that debate.
