# Hong Kong — Parental Alienation Legal Reference

> Jurisdiction-specific legal reference for parents, lawyers, and clinicians navigating parental-alienation matters in Hong Kong. Cross-referenced with deeply-investigated case studies. CC BY 4.0.

## Quick reference

| Element | Hong Kong position |
|---|---|
| Statutory PA recognition | No. Family law operates on the English common-law template: *Matrimonial Causes Ordinance* (Cap. 179), *Matrimonial Proceedings and Property Ordinance* (Cap. 192), *Guardianship of Minors Ordinance* (Cap. 13), *Parent and Child Ordinance* (Cap. 429). *Welfare of the child first and paramount.* |
| Apex court direction (current) | The Court of Appeal in [H v W [2021] HKCA 733](../case-studies/h-v-w-2021-hkca-733-hong-kong-court-appeal-parental-alienation-relocation.md) treated parental alienation as a recognised concept in a relocation context and applied English authority (including *Re S (Parental Alienation: Cult)* [2020] EWCA Civ 568) as persuasive. |
| Standard of proof for PA findings | Civil standard (balance of probabilities); welfare of the child as the first and paramount consideration under GMO s.3. |
| Forensic-evaluator framework | International Social Service (ISS) reports; *Social Investigation Reports* (SIRs) ordered by the Family Court; *Single Joint Experts* (clinical psychologists) under the Court's direction. |
| Reunification programs court-ordered | Therapeutic interventions through clinical psychologists; *Children's Cases Procedure* (CCP) under PD15.13; *Family Mediation Co-ordinator's Office*. |
| Domestic-abuse counterweight in place | *Domestic and Cohabitation Relationships Violence Ordinance* (Cap. 189); *Crimes Ordinance* offences; *Domestic Violence Database* (Social Welfare Department). |

## Statutory framework

- **Guardianship of Minors Ordinance (Cap. 13)** — substantive child-welfare framework; s.3 sets welfare of the minor as first and paramount. [Hong Kong eLegislation](https://www.elegislation.gov.hk/hk/cap13).
- **Matrimonial Causes Ordinance (Cap. 179)** — divorce jurisdiction and ancillary children matters. [HKeL](https://www.elegislation.gov.hk/hk/cap179).
- **Matrimonial Proceedings and Property Ordinance (Cap. 192)** — financial and property relief, with adjacent custody powers. [HKeL](https://www.elegislation.gov.hk/hk/cap192).
- **Parent and Child Ordinance (Cap. 429)** — paternity and parental status framework. [HKeL](https://www.elegislation.gov.hk/hk/cap429).
- **Domestic and Cohabitation Relationships Violence Ordinance (Cap. 189)** — DV injunctions, including residence and contact provisions. [HKeL](https://www.elegislation.gov.hk/hk/cap189).
- **Practice Direction 15.13 — Children's Cases Procedure** — pilot CCP framework for child-related family proceedings. [Judiciary — Practice Directions](https://legalref.judiciary.hk/lrs/common/pd/pdlist.jsp).

## Apex court direction

The leading reference is **[H v W [2021] HKCA 733 (24 May 2021)](../case-studies/h-v-w-2021-hkca-733-hong-kong-court-appeal-parental-alienation-relocation.md)**, decided by Lam VP, Yuen JA, and B Chu J:

- The Court of Appeal of the High Court of the HKSAR considered a mother's application for leave to relocate a five-year-old child from Hong Kong to Singapore, in the context of disputed allegations of parental alienation against the father.
- The Court applied the English template, including *Payne v Payne* relocation jurisprudence (as developed in *K v K (Relocation: Shared Care Arrangement)* and recent E&W lines) and the English PA case-law, treating *Re S (Parental Alienation: Cult)* [2020] EWCA Civ 568 as persuasive.
- The decision is the first Hong Kong Court of Appeal relocation case to consider PA in the COVID-era travel-ban context, and confirms the operational status of PA as a recognised concept in Hong Kong family-court reasoning.
- Reported on HKLII: [H v W [2021] HKCA 733 — HKLII](https://www.hklii.hk/en/cases/hkca/2021/733). Case digest: [Hong Kong Lawyer — H v W](https://www.hk-lawyer.org/content/h-v-w).

The **Court of Final Appeal (CFA)** is the HKSAR apex; there is no CFA PA-specific authority that supersedes H v W. The HKCA's treatment is consolidated within the High Court appellate structure.

## Standard of proof

Hong Kong civil procedure operates on the balance of probabilities. The substantive child-welfare standard is the *welfare of the minor* as the *first and paramount* consideration under GMO s.3. This mirrors the English template and admits the English line of PA jurisprudence as persuasive guidance.

## Forensic evaluation framework

- **International Social Service (ISS) Hong Kong** — provides Social Investigation Reports (SIRs) to the Family Court in cross-jurisdictional and relocation matters.
- **Social Welfare Department (SWD)** — preparation of SIRs and welfare assessments.
- **Single Joint Expert (SJE)** — typically a clinical psychologist instructed jointly by the parties under the Court's direction.
- **Practice Direction 15.13 (Children's Cases Procedure)** — encourages early identification of welfare issues and use of multidisciplinary input.
- **Separate Representation of Children** — available in defined cases under the Court's direction, drawing on the English template.

## Reunification programs

- Court-ordered therapeutic interventions through clinical psychologists and counselling services.
- **Family Mediation Co-ordinator's Office** — Court-affiliated mediation infrastructure.
- **Supervised access** through SWD or accredited NGOs.
- US/Anglo-style coercive residential reunification camps are not part of the Hong Kong toolkit.

## Domestic-violence interaction

- **DCRVO (Cap. 189)** — civil injunction regime including residence and contact provisions; available against intimate partners and household members.
- **Crimes Ordinance** — criminal offences relevant to DV (common assault, criminal intimidation, etc.).
- **Social Welfare Department Family and Child Protective Services Unit** — child-protection intervention where care concerns arise.
- The HKCA in H v W acknowledges that allegations of harm must be evaluated on their merits; the PA framework does not displace independent evaluation of DV/CSA allegations.

## Honest gaps

- Court of Final Appeal jurisprudence specifically addressing PA is **not publicly confirmed** in this reference.
- The interaction between the *Children's Cases Procedure* pilot (PD 15.13) and standard family-court procedure is uneven across registries; consolidated outcome data is not public.
- Hong Kong's adoption of English PA jurisprudence post *Re H-N* and others [2021] EWCA Civ 448 (which significantly tightened the English approach) has not been authoritatively addressed.
- Empirical research on PA-allegation prevalence in Hong Kong family-court matters is sparse.
