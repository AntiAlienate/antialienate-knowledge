# Germany — Parental Alienation Legal Reference

> Jurisdiction-specific legal reference for parents, lawyers, and clinicians navigating parental-alienation matters in Germany. Cross-referenced with deeply-investigated case studies. CC BY 4.0.

## Quick reference

| Element | Germany position |
|---|---|
| Statutory PA recognition | No. The Bürgerliches Gesetzbuch (BGB) does not use the term *Eltern-Kind-Entfremdung*, *elterliche Entfremdung*, or PAS. Family-court interventions run through § 1666 BGB (Kindeswohlgefährdung) and § 1671 BGB (transfer of *Aufenthaltsbestimmungsrecht*). |
| Apex court direction (current) | Sceptical. The Bundesverfassungsgericht in [1 BvR 1076/23 (17 November 2023)](../case-studies/bverfg-1-bvr-1076-23-eltern-kind-entfremdung-2023.md) annulled an OLG Köln residence-transfer order founded on an *Eltern-Kind-Entfremdung* construction and emphasised that the construct is scientifically contested and cannot, on its own, justify the most invasive parental-rights interventions. |
| Standard of proof for PA findings | Civil standard (richterliche Überzeugung) under § 286 ZPO; § 1666 BGB orders additionally require a *konkrete Gefahr* (concrete endangerment) of the child's welfare. |
| Forensic-evaluator framework | Court-appointed *Sachverständige* under § 163 FamFG, bound by the BGH/BVerfG-derived methodological minima (Mindestanforderungen). Verfahrensbeistand under § 158 FamFG represents the child's interests independently. |
| Reunification programs court-ordered | *Umgangspflegschaft* (§ 1684 IV BGB) is the principal tool; residential or coerced "reunification camps" are not part of the standard German toolkit. |
| Domestic-abuse counterweight in place | Gewaltschutzgesetz (GewSchG) protective orders run in parallel with family-court proceedings; the BVerfG has been increasingly explicit that DV allegations must be assessed before invasive contact-enforcement steps. |

## Statutory framework

- **§ 1666 BGB — gerichtliche Maßnahmen bei Gefährdung des Kindeswohls.** The constitutional gateway to all invasive family-court intervention. Full text: [Gesetze im Internet](https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/bgb/__1666.html).
- **§ 1671 BGB — Übertragung der elterlichen Sorge bei Getrenntleben der Eltern.** Governs transfer of parental authority, including the *Aufenthaltsbestimmungsrecht* (right to determine the child's residence). [Gesetze im Internet](https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/bgb/__1671.html).
- **§ 1684 BGB — Umgang des Kindes mit den Eltern.** The contact regime, including the duty of each parent not to impair the child's relationship with the other parent (Wohlverhaltensgebot). [Gesetze im Internet](https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/bgb/__1684.html).
- **§ 158 FamFG — Verfahrensbeistand.** The child's procedural representative in family-court proceedings. [Gesetze im Internet](https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/famfg/__158.html).
- **§ 163 FamFG — Sachverständigengutachten.** Court-appointed expert reports in family-court matters. [Gesetze im Internet](https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/famfg/__163.html).
- **§ 89 FamFG — Ordnungsmittel** (Ordnungsgeld, Ordnungshaft) for breach of contact orders. [Gesetze im Internet](https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/famfg/__89.html).
- **Gewaltschutzgesetz (GewSchG)** — protection-order regime in domestic-violence cases. [Gesetze im Internet](https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/gewschg/).
- **Art. 6 Abs. 2 GG** — constitutional parental right (Elterngrundrecht). [Bundestag GG text](https://www.bundestag.de/gg).

## Apex court direction

The leading recent statement is **[BVerfG, Kammerbeschluss vom 17.11.2023 – 1 BvR 1076/23](../case-studies/bverfg-1-bvr-1076-23-eltern-kind-entfremdung-2023.md)**. The 2. Kammer of the First Senate set aside an OLG Köln order transferring *Aufenthaltsbestimmungsrecht* from the primary-carer mother to the father on an *Eltern-Kind-Entfremdung* (EKE) construction. Key constitutional propositions:

1. Transfer of residence-determination right is among the most invasive family-court interventions and engages Art. 6 Abs. 2 S. 1 GG at its core; courts must make an autonomous assessment of *Kindeswohl* and may not collapse that assessment into a contested clinical label.
2. EKE / PAS is treated by the BVerfG as a scientifically disputed construct (*wissenschaftlich umstritten*); a court may not premise an invasive order on the construct without engaging with its evidentiary basis and contradictory clinical literature.
3. The lower court's procedural treatment of the mother's *Anhörungsrüge* additionally violated Art. 103 Abs. 1 GG.

The Bundesgerichtshof (BGH), in family-law decisions of the XII. Zivilsenat, has historically required that expert opinion in contested custody and contact cases be measured against the BGH/BVerfG-derived *Mindestanforderungen an die Qualität von Sachverständigengutachten im Kindschaftsrecht* (originally promulgated by Salzgeber et al.; cited approvingly in BGH XII ZB jurisprudence — **exact BGH cite not publicly confirmed** in this reference).

## Standard of proof

German civil procedure does not use the Anglo-American "preponderance" / "clear and convincing" tiers. The family court must reach *richterliche Überzeugung* (judicial conviction) under § 286 ZPO. For an order under § 1666 BGB the court must additionally find a *konkrete Gefahr* (concrete, not merely speculative, endangerment) of the child's welfare. Post-BVerfG 1 BvR 1076/23, that threshold cannot be met by a bare expert opinion labelling the case "EKE"; the court must independently identify the conduct, link it to identifiable harm, and rule out less invasive remedies.

## Forensic evaluation framework

- Sachverständige are appointed by the court under § 163 FamFG and are bound by the *Mindestanforderungen an die Qualität von Sachverständigengutachten im Kindschaftsrecht* (cited in the standard family-law commentaries: MüKo-BGB; Staudinger; FamRZ jurisprudence).
- Verfahrensbeistand under § 158 FamFG is the child's own procedural representative and is independent of the parents and the youth-welfare office (Jugendamt).
- Jugendamt participation is mandatory under § 162 FamFG; the Jugendamt's report (Stellungnahme) does not bind the court but is routinely influential.
- Post-BVerfG 1 BvR 1076/23, family courts that adopt an EKE diagnosis from a Gutachten without independently testing the methodology face elevated reversal risk on Verfassungsbeschwerde.

## Reunification programs

- **Umgangspflegschaft** under § 1684 Abs. 3 BGB — a court-appointed Umgangspfleger supervises and facilitates contact, including handling handovers and reporting back to the court. This is the principal German judicial tool for re-establishing impaired contact and is the standard alternative to coercive residence transfer.
- Therapeutic and family-mediation services through the Jugendamt and Beratungsstellen (counselling services) under §§ 17, 18 SGB VIII (Sozialgesetzbuch VIII / Kinder- und Jugendhilfegesetz). [Gesetze im Internet — SGB VIII](https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/sgb_8/).
- US/Anglo-style "reunification camps" or coercive residential placements are not part of the standard German toolkit and would be doctrinally difficult under Art. 6 GG and the BVerfG's recent line.

## Domestic-violence interaction

DV allegations are litigated through the **Gewaltschutzgesetz (GewSchG)** and run in parallel with family-court proceedings. Family courts are required to take DV allegations seriously before making contact or transfer orders, and the BVerfG's recent line (1 BvR 1076/23 and adjacent decisions) makes clear that an EKE construction may not be used to neutralise DV allegations. Germany has ratified the Council of Europe's **Istanbul Convention** (in force for Germany 1 February 2018); the GREVIO baseline evaluation report flagged family-court treatment of DV allegations as an area requiring improvement.

## Honest gaps

- BGH XII ZB jurisprudence on family-court Gutachten methodology is paraphrased here; exact pinpoint BGH citations are **not publicly confirmed** in this reference and should be checked against MüKo-BGB or FamRZ before being relied on.
- Empirical data on prevalence of EKE-labelled orders in German family courts is sparse; the Bundesministerium der Justiz has not published a systematic study.
- Post-1 BvR 1076/23 OLG response patterns (whether OLGs have changed their use of EKE language) are not yet captured in published empirical work.
