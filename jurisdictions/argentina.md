# Argentina — Parental Alienation Legal Reference

> Jurisdiction-specific legal reference for parents, lawyers, and clinicians navigating parental-alienation matters in Argentina. Cross-referenced with deeply-investigated case studies. CC BY 4.0.

## Quick reference

| Element | Argentina position |
|---|---|
| Statutory PA recognition | Indirect. The Código Civil y Comercial (CCyC, 2015) does not use the term *alienación parental* in its substantive provisions, but the *responsabilidad parental* regime (arts. 638 ff.) and the *plan de parentalidad* / *cuidado personal* framework reach the conduct. **Ley 24.270 (1993)** criminalises *impedimento de contacto* of non-custodial parents. |
| Apex court direction (current) | The CSJN's 7 October 2021 *P. B., E. G. c/ B., K. E. s/ Medidas Precautorias* (CSJ 1813/2018/RH1) is treated by leading commentators (Mizrahi) as the first plena admisión of *alienación parental* by the CSJN. See [case study](../case-studies/csjn-p-b-e-g-c-b-k-e-medidas-precautorias-2021-alienacion-parental.md). |
| Standard of proof for PA findings | Civil standard (*sana crítica*) under arts. 161 ff. CPCCN; *interés superior del niño* under art. 3 CRC, art. 75 inc. 22 CN, and art. 3 of Ley 26.061. |
| Forensic-evaluator framework | Court-appointed *perito psicólogo* per CPCCN arts. 457 ff.; *Cuerpo Médico Forense* in federal/national jurisdiction; *equipos interdisciplinarios* of the *Justicia de Familia*. |
| Reunification programs court-ordered | *Régimen de comunicación asistido* and *centros de revinculación*; SCBA's 9 August 2024 follow-up to *P. B.* orders progressive *revinculación* with therapeutic support. |
| Domestic-abuse counterweight in place | Yes. **Ley 26.485** (protección integral a las mujeres); **Ley 26.061** (protección integral de niñas, niños y adolescentes); *Convención de Belém do Pará*. |

## Statutory framework

- **Constitución Nacional, art. 75 inc. 22** — constitutional rank of the CRC and other human-rights treaties. [InfoLEG — CN](https://servicios.infoleg.gob.ar/infolegInternet/anexos/0-4999/804/norma.htm).
- **Código Civil y Comercial de la Nación (CCyC, Ley 26.994, 2015)**:
  - Arts. 638–704 — *responsabilidad parental*. [InfoLEG — CCyC](https://servicios.infoleg.gob.ar/infolegInternet/anexos/235000-239999/235975/norma.htm).
  - Art. 651 — *cuidado personal compartido indistinto* as the default rule.
  - Art. 655 — *plan de parentalidad*.
- **Ley 24.270, de 3 de noviembre de 1993** — *Impedimento de contacto de los hijos menores con sus padres no convivientes*. Criminal sanctions for the resident parent who obstructs contact. [InfoLEG — Ley 24.270](https://servicios.infoleg.gob.ar/infolegInternet/anexos/0-4999/675/norma.htm).
- **Ley 26.061, de 21 de octubre de 2005** — *Protección integral de los derechos de las niñas, niños y adolescentes*. [InfoLEG — Ley 26.061](https://servicios.infoleg.gob.ar/infolegInternet/anexos/110000-114999/110778/norma.htm).
- **Ley 26.485, de 14 de abril de 2009** — *Protección integral para prevenir, sancionar y erradicar la violencia contra las mujeres*. [InfoLEG — Ley 26.485](https://servicios.infoleg.gob.ar/infolegInternet/anexos/150000-154999/152155/norma.htm).
- **Convención sobre los Derechos del Niño** — incorporated at constitutional rank via art. 75 inc. 22 CN.

## Apex court direction

The leading apex-court reference is **[P. B., E. G. c/ B., K. E. s/ Medidas Precautorias (CSJN, 7 October 2021, CSJ 1813/2018/RH1)](../case-studies/csjn-p-b-e-g-c-b-k-e-medidas-precautorias-2021-alienacion-parental.md)**, followed by the **Suprema Corte de Justicia de la Provincia de Buenos Aires** sentence of 9 August 2024 in C. 121.539 (Kogan ponente).

- The CSJN granted the father's *recurso de hecho* against an SCBA decision that had failed to adequately consider the *interés superior del niño* in the context of *medidas precautorias* directed at the mother's obstruction of contact with the three children.
- Mauricio Luis Mizrahi (*La Ley*, 12/4/2022, AR/DOC/1227/2022) characterised the ruling as the *plena admisión de la alienación parental por la justicia nacional* — the first Argentine federal apex-court endorsement of the doctrine.
- The SCBA's 9 August 2024 sentence (Kogan opening; Torres; Violini; Mancini) on remand articulated a phased *revinculación* with therapeutic support, accepting the CSJN's framing.
- Practitioner commentary at *Colectivo Derecho de Familia* and at ASAPMI (Asociación Argentina de Prevención del Maltrato Infanto-Juvenil) has tracked subsequent provincial-court applications.

## Standard of proof

The CCyC and CPCCN operate under *sana crítica* in the evaluation of evidence. Family-court orders affecting *responsabilidad parental* or *cuidado personal* are decided under *interés superior del niño* (CRC art. 3, Ley 26.061 art. 3, CCyC art. 706 c) and *tutela judicial efectiva*. There is no separate "clear and convincing" tier.

## Forensic evaluation framework

- **Perito psicólogo / perito psiquiatra** under CPCCN arts. 457 ff. — court-appointed or party-proposed expert evidence.
- **Cuerpo Médico Forense (CMF)** of the Corte Suprema de Justicia de la Nación — official forensic resource for federal/national jurisdiction.
- **Equipos interdisciplinarios** attached to *Tribunales de Familia* / *Juzgados de Familia* (provincial-jurisdiction variable).
- **Abogado del niño** under Ley 26.061 art. 27 and CCyC art. 26 — child's own counsel in proceedings affecting personal rights.
- **Ministerio Público de la Defensa** intervention in all proceedings concerning *niños, niñas y adolescentes*.

## Reunification programs

- **Régimen de comunicación asistido / supervisado** — court-ordered supervised contact, typically through provincial *centros de revinculación* or NGO providers.
- **Terapia de revinculación** — court-ordered therapeutic reconnection processes; SCBA 9 August 2024 articulates a representative phased model.
- **Mediación familiar** — *Ley 26.589 (2010)* on national mediation; *Centros Públicos de Mediación* operate at federal and provincial level.
- US/Anglo-style coercive residential reunification camps are not part of the Argentine toolkit; the *interés superior del niño* and Convention rights would constrain such interventions.

## Domestic-violence interaction

- **Ley 26.485** is the principal federal DV framework; *medidas de protección* are emergency civil measures available to *Justicia de Familia*.
- **Ley 26.061** governs child-welfare interventions and integrates with the DV framework.
- **Convención de Belém do Pará** ratified by Argentina; CEVI follow-up.
- CSJN's 7-X-2021 ruling acknowledges that family courts must balance DV considerations with the *interés superior del niño* when evaluating contact obstruction; the doctrine does not displace DV-protective measures where independently substantiated.

## Honest gaps

- The precise composition of the CSJN panel that signed the 7-X-2021 sentencia in CSJ 1813/2018/RH1 is **not publicly confirmed** in indexed extracts.
- Provincial-court application of the *P. B.* doctrine outside Buenos Aires is uneven; this reference does not map per-province application.
- The interaction between Ley 26.485 *medidas de protección* and Ley 24.270 *impedimento de contacto* prosecutions is a live area with little consolidated appellate guidance.
