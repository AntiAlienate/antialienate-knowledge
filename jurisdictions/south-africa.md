# South Africa — Parental Alienation Legal Reference

> Jurisdiction-specific legal reference for parents, lawyers, and clinicians navigating parental-alienation matters in South Africa. Cross-referenced with deeply-investigated case studies. CC BY 4.0.

## Quick reference

| Element | South Africa position |
|---|---|
| Statutory PA recognition | No. The *Children's Act 38 of 2005* does not use the term *parental alienation*. The Act's operative concepts are *parental responsibilities and rights* (ss. 18–22), *best interests of the child* (s. 7), and *child participation* (s. 10). The High Court sits as *upper guardian* of all minors. |
| Apex court direction (current) | The High Court has reasoned extensively in PA terms in [T.L.D v B.G [2023] ZAGPJHC 801 (Adams J)](../case-studies/tld-v-bg-2023-zagpjhc-801-south-africa-parental-alienation.md), drawing on Children's Act framework and Constitution s.28(2). The Constitutional Court has not issued a PA-specific judgment. |
| Standard of proof for PA findings | Civil standard (balance of probabilities); *best interests of the child* under Constitution s.28(2) is the *paramount* substantive standard. |
| Forensic-evaluator framework | Court-appointed *clinical psychologists* and *curators ad litem*; *Family Advocate* under the Mediation in Certain Divorce Matters Act 24 of 1987; *children's court* investigations under the Children's Act. |
| Reunification programs court-ordered | Therapeutic reconnection processes through clinical psychologists; *parenting coordinators* (developed through High Court jurisprudence, e.g., *MB v NB* line); supervised contact through accredited NGOs. |
| Domestic-abuse counterweight in place | Yes. *Domestic Violence Act 116 of 1998* (substantially amended 2021); *Protection from Harassment Act 17 of 2011*; *Sexual Offences Act 32 of 2007*. |

## Statutory framework

- **Constitution of the Republic of South Africa, 1996**:
  - s.28(2) — "*A child's best interests are of paramount importance in every matter concerning the child.*" [Justice — Constitution](https://www.justice.gov.za/legislation/constitution/SAConstitution-web-eng.pdf).
  - s.28(1)(b) — every child has the right to family care, parental care, or appropriate alternative care.
- **Children's Act 38 of 2005**:
  - s. 7 — best-interests-of-the-child factors. [SAFLII / Justice — Children's Act](https://www.justice.gov.za/legislation/acts/2005-038%20childrensact.pdf).
  - s. 10 — child participation.
  - ss. 18–22 — parental responsibilities and rights.
  - s. 23–24 — assignment of contact and care by court order.
  - s. 31 — major decisions affecting the child.
- **Mediation in Certain Divorce Matters Act 24 of 1987** — Family Advocate framework. [SAFLII / Justice](https://www.justice.gov.za/legislation/acts/1987-024.pdf).
- **Divorce Act 70 of 1979** — divorce jurisdiction with adjacent child provisions. [Justice](https://www.justice.gov.za/legislation/acts/1979-070.pdf).
- **Domestic Violence Act 116 of 1998** (amended by Act 14 of 2021) — DV protection-order regime. [Justice](https://www.justice.gov.za/legislation/acts/1998-116.pdf).
- **Hague Convention on the Civil Aspects of International Child Abduction Act 72 of 1996** — incorporates Hague Convention.

## Apex court direction

The leading reference is **[T.L.D v B.G (015642/2022) [2023] ZAGPJHC 801 (13 July 2023), Adams J, Gauteng Local Division](../case-studies/tld-v-bg-2023-zagpjhc-801-south-africa-parental-alienation.md)** (with parallel citation ZAGPJHC 821):

- Adams J, sitting in the High Court as *upper guardian* of minors, gave one of the most extensively reasoned PA-framed judgments in South African case-law.
- The Court relied on Children's Act s.7 (best-interests factors) and Constitution s.28(2) and drew on the expert evidence of Mr Leonard Carr (court-appointed clinical psychologist) and Advocate Mark Leonard Haskins SC (curator ad litem).
- The Court accepted findings of moderate PA directed at the mother by the father and structured contact and therapeutic interventions accordingly, while emphasising that PA is a descriptor of family-system dysfunction rather than a syndromic diagnosis.
- The judgment is reported at: [SAFLII — [2023] ZAGPJHC 801](https://www.saflii.org/za/cases/ZAGPJHC/2023/801.html); leave-to-appeal: [SAFLII — [2023] ZAGPJHC 872](https://www.saflii.org/za/cases/ZAGPJHC/2023/872.html); parallel: [Law Library — [2023] ZAGPJHC 821](https://lawlibrary.org.za/akn/za-gp/judgment/zagpjhc/2023/821/eng@2023-07-13).

The **Supreme Court of Appeal** and **Constitutional Court** have not issued PA-specific judgments; doctrinal authority sits at the High Court level, drawing on the Children's Act framework and Constitution s.28(2).

## Standard of proof

South African civil procedure operates on the balance of probabilities. The substantive child-welfare standard is the *paramount* importance of the child's best interests under Constitution s.28(2), elaborated in Children's Act s.7. There is no separate "clear and convincing" tier for PA findings.

## Forensic evaluation framework

- **Family Advocate** under the Mediation in Certain Divorce Matters Act 24 of 1987 — official adviser to the court on the best interests of the child in divorce and related proceedings.
- **Curator ad litem** — appointed by the High Court in its upper-guardian capacity to represent the child's interests; can be a senior advocate (as Adv. Mark Haskins SC in T.L.D v B.G).
- **Court-appointed clinical psychologist** — instructed under the High Court's case-management powers; report submitted to the Court and parties.
- **Children's Court investigations** under the Children's Act — designated social workers prepare reports.
- **Office of the Family Advocate** — statewide infrastructure under the Department of Justice and Constitutional Development.

## Reunification programs

- Court-ordered therapeutic reconnection processes through clinical psychologists.
- **Parenting coordinators** — developed through High Court jurisprudence (notably *MB v NB* and the *Town v Town* line); operate by consent or court order.
- **Supervised contact** through accredited NGOs and contact centres.
- US/Anglo-style coercive residential reunification camps are not part of the South African toolkit and would face constitutional scrutiny under s.28(2) and s.12 (freedom and security of the person).

## Domestic-violence interaction

- **Domestic Violence Act 116 of 1998** (amended 2021) — protection-order regime, including provisions affecting residence and contact.
- **Protection from Harassment Act 17 of 2011** — broader harassment-order regime.
- **Children's Court** under the Children's Act — child-protection jurisdiction with care-plan and place-of-safety powers.
- T.L.D v B.G acknowledges that the PA framework does not displace independent evaluation of harm allegations; the *best interests* standard requires the Court to address both PA and harm allegations on their evidentiary merits.

## Honest gaps

- Constitutional Court treatment of PA framings is **not publicly confirmed** in any reported judgment; doctrinal authority sits at High Court level.
- The interaction between the Domestic Violence Act (as amended in 2021) and PA-framed High Court proceedings has not produced a definitive appellate doctrine.
- Parenting coordinator practice varies across divisions of the High Court; consolidated outcome data is not public.
- T.L.D v B.G's reception by other High Court divisions (Western Cape, KwaZulu-Natal, Eastern Cape) is uneven; this reference does not map divisional uptake.
